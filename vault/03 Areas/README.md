# Areas

Ongoing responsibilities with no finish line.

Areas are things you maintain over time: health, finances, home, business operations, brand, learning, systems, or the Life OS itself.

## Project vs Area

- **Area:** ongoing responsibility, standard, or domain.
- **Project:** time-bound effort that improves or serves an Area.

Example: “Health” is an Area. “Run a 10K in April” is a Project that belongs under Projects and links back to Health.

## Suggested naming

Use names that make the folder list easy to scan.

Optional prefix pattern:

```text
Business - Operations
Life - Health
Money - Tax
Systems - Digital HQ
```

The exact prefixes are yours. Keep them consistent once chosen.

## Area shape

Each important area should have a folder:

```text
03 Areas/<Area Name>/
├── README.md
└── Log.md          # optional but useful for active areas
```

### Area README minimum

- Purpose: what this area is responsible for.
- What belongs here.
- Current state or standards.
- Recurring rhythms or review cadence.
- Related projects.
- Key resources or tools.

### Area Log

Use `Log.md` for dated progress, decisions, reviews, and changes to standards.

## What belongs here

- Standards and recurring responsibilities.
- Current operating context for an ongoing domain.
- Links to related projects and resources.
- Maintenance notes and decisions.

## What doesn't belong

- One-off efforts with a finish line → `01 Projects/`.
- Reference material with no operational role → `04 Resources/`.
- Person-specific relationship notes → `02 People/`.

## Agent instructions

- Preserve long-term context.
- Create or recommend a Project when the work has a finish line.
- Do not bury project tasks inside an Area README.
- Update Area logs when standards, recurring workflows, or important decisions change.

## Archive

Inactive areas move to:

```text
05 Archive/Areas Archive/
```

Do not delete areas just because they are quiet.
