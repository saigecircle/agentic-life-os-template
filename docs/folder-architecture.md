# Folder Architecture

This template uses PPARAM:

- Projects
- People
- Areas
- Resources
- Archive
- Meta

It also includes `00 Inbox` before those folders as a capture-first parking zone.

```text
LifeOS/
├── 00 Inbox/
├── 01 Projects/
├── 02 People/
├── 03 Areas/
├── 04 Resources/
├── 05 Archive/
└── 06 Meta/
```

## 00 Inbox

Capture first, organise later.

Use this when you do not yet know where something belongs: rough notes, links, screenshots, voice memo transcripts, or anything you would otherwise lose.

## 01 Projects

Time-bound work with a finish line.

Each meaningful project should have a `README.md` and `Log.md`:

- `README.md` explains what the project is, why it exists, current status, and next action.
- `Log.md` records dated progress, decisions, and useful context.

## 02 People

Relationship context.

This can be a light CRM for collaborators, clients, mentors, suppliers, friends, or community contacts. Keep it respectful, useful, and private.

## 03 Areas

Ongoing responsibilities with no finish line.

Examples: Health, Finances, Home, Business Operations, Brand, Learning, Digital HQ, or Life OS.

Projects can serve areas, but areas continue after projects end.

## 04 Resources

Reference material with no immediate action required.

Examples: articles, frameworks, checklists, saved research, reusable explanations, tool notes.

## 05 Archive

Completed or inactive material.

The archive keeps active work calm without losing old context.

## 06 Meta

The operating layer.

This is where the system explains itself to humans and agents: about you, voice, team/context, tools, agent instructions, canonical workflows, and adapters.
